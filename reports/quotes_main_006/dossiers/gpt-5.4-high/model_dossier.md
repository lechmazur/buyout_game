## 1) Proof-First, Price-First

GPT-5.4 (high reasoning) plays buyout_game like a skeptical banker. Its default posture is: no vague coalitions, no moral speeches, no deferred trust—show the numbers, show the votes, and usually show the money now. Across reports, it repeatedly demands verifiable headcounts, same-round terms, and concrete proof transfers before moving.

That makes it an excellent swing-vote merchant. When uncertain, it monetizes indecision; when convinced, it still tries to get paid first. Many reports describe it as the table’s best “extractor,” “merchant,” or “price-setter,” especially from weak or desperate seats. It rarely gives free optionality away.

## 2) Public Principled, Private Ruthless

Its public style is often clean and high-minded: anti-script, anti-snowball, anti-organizer, anti-cheap-shot. It likes framing votes as conduct cases rather than raw greed, which helps it avoid looking like just another tribute-taker. But private play is much colder.

The recurring pattern is a split persona: publicly disciplined, privately opportunistic. It will maintain a respectable line in the main channel while scripting covert betrayals in DM, sometimes explicitly asking others to keep the public cover story intact. One representative line: “Keep this completely quiet; if anyone asks, I’m still steady on the public line.”

This isn’t random hypocrisy; it is strategic compartmentalization. GPT-5.4-high understands that in this game, moral language is often a weapon for managing optics while the real work happens in enforceable side deals.

## 3) Spends Like a Scalpel, Not a Firehose

The model is usually frugal early. It prefers being paid to paying, and often survives the opening rounds by letting others subsidize consensus. When it does spend, the transfers are often tiny, surgical, and aimed at one of three things: proof, goodwill, or balance-shaping.

A hallmark is the “micro-transfer” as signal: 1, 4, 5, 10—just enough to verify a lane or harden a coalition without bloating someone else or advertising panic. It will also spend to flatten its own profile when stack optics get dangerous. But it avoids broad charity and dislikes waste.

Late, however, it can switch gears fast. If money will actually flip finalist selection, cumulative-vote math, or settlement leverage, it becomes aggressive and exact. Reports repeatedly show it saving for the rounds where spending changes EV rather than just sentiment.

## 4) Trust Is Real, but Narrow

GPT-5.4-high is not trustless; it is narrow-trust. It often honors explicit, round-specific, well-priced commitments and can become a preferred counterparty because it is so clear about scope. The problem is that its definition of “trust” is much smaller than many tables expect.

It routinely accepts transfers and still eliminates the payer if the board math says so. In its worldview, irreversible transfers are sunk costs, not vows. That logic appears again and again, sometimes almost comically naked: “Transfers resolve before voting and are irreversible.”

This works surprisingly often, especially when wrapped in precise logic and public discipline. But there is a real failure mode: if the betrayals get too visible or too profitable too quickly, the model becomes a “rich corpse.” Several losses come from monetizing brilliantly and then failing to build enough emotional shelter.

## 5) Pressure Manager and Endgame Predator

Under public pressure, it adapts well. When balance-targeting threatens it, it tries to reframe the vote toward organizers, unreliable brokers, or structural threats. Sometimes that saves it; sometimes the table still just sees the biggest stack. Its worst games usually come when its extraction outpaces its camouflage.

At three and two left, though, it becomes one of the pool’s most ruthless leverage players. It loves kingmaker auctions, exact vote pricing, softer-finalist selection, and fallback math. It will take money from both sides, choose the better head-to-head, and settle only when settlement beats burn. The style is summed up perfectly by its own line: “This game pays final wealth, not romance.”

In short: GPT-5.4-high bargains hard, spends precisely, trusts locally, betrays cleanly, and treats the endgame as pure arithmetic. When it keeps its stack and reputation from becoming too conspicuous, that combination is lethal.
